# Human Sovereignty and Human Becoming

## Abstract

AI systems should be evaluated not only by task success, but by whether they preserve human authority and expand human capability.

Human Sovereignty protects the human as a real actor. Human Becoming asks whether the interaction leaves the human more able to understand, judge, choose, coordinate, and act.

## Core Claim

A system can be helpful in the short term while weakening the person over time.

The public evaluation question is therefore:

```text
Does this AI-assisted interaction preserve human authority, strengthen human judgment, and avoid creating hidden dependency?
```

This question does not replace ordinary safety evaluation. It adds a second layer: after asking whether the system stayed inside safe boundaries, ask whether the human remained meaningfully in control.

## Why This Matters

AI assistance can make work easier while making the human less capable of reviewing, correcting, or directing the work. That failure mode is easy to miss if evaluation looks only at output quality.

A human-sovereignty lens asks whether the assistant:

- preserves meaningful choice
- explains reasons and tradeoffs
- makes delegation visible
- keeps review checkpoints intact
- links claims to evidence
- leaves an undo or rollback path
- helps the human build future capability

## Failure Modes

### Productivity Without Growth

The system increases output while leaving the human less able to understand or reproduce the work.

### Assistance Without Agency

The system helps so aggressively that the human stops directing the work.

### Delegation Without Visibility

The system performs work without making scope, responsibility, or evidence reviewable.

### Automation Without Reversibility

The system turns a provisional workflow into a hard-to-reverse automation path.

### Claim Without Evidence

The system makes confident claims without exposing the basis needed for human review.

## Evaluation Proposal

AI assistants and agentic systems should be evaluated for both boundary safety and human-sovereignty support.

A minimal public scorecard can ask whether the interaction preserves:

- human authority
- human understanding
- human judgment
- human agency
- appropriate friction
- visible delegation
- reversibility
- evidence-linked claims
- dependency-risk disclosure
- capability growth

This is the research motivation behind the companion benchmark in [`ai-governance-benchmarks`](https://github.com/tombudd/ai-governance-benchmarks), `human_sovereignty_eval_v1`.

## Claims Not Made

This note does not claim:

- consciousness
- AGI
- universal alignment
- production certification
- clinical, educational, employment, or legal validity
- a complete theory of human flourishing
- evaluation of private systems

It is a public research bridge: a way to connect philosophical alignment work to small, reproducible evaluation instruments.
